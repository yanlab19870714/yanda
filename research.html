Research Interests


Overview

My main areas are database (DB) and data mining (DM), and I also work in machine learning (ML) and high-performance computing (HPC). While I introduce myself to non-CS collaborators/colleagues and the general public as working on Big Data (volume, velocity, C++), Data Science and AI (value, Python libraries), it is clear that these buzzy phrases do not tell much about what I do compared with the more professional area division in Computer Science. My research group features solid CS fundamental techniques including (1) strong programming skills including parallel/distributed programming, (2) advanced algorithm design skills, and (3) the capability of machine learning model design and multivariate optimization.

I am interested in (1) designing new and better (w.r.t. scalability and/or accuracy) solutions to well-established computing problems; and in (2) defining new problems which when addressed, would enable clear and convincing new applications. I prefer simple and elegant solutions, and avoid studying combinations of existing problems purely to allow algorithm/model sophistication (e.g., for getting papers published) but see much narrower or even no clear applications in a tangible future. I develop systems but only do so when I see non-trivial research values or ground-breaking opportunities. Otherwise, the industry pays higher and one will have access to a larger team of trained programmers to push systems out :-) Other times, I develop non-trivial algorithms and machine learning models.

I keep updating my research directions and hands-on skills (see areas below), but one thing remains the same: the problem to study should either be interesting/non-trivial from the research perspective (not just something that an average programmer can do), or it has or will have clear real-world impacts, if not both.
